    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Frost Death Knight, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Frost Death Knight in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Frost Death Knight in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Frost Death Knight gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Frost Death Knight drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        The BiS gear for Frost is much closer between the competitive talent builds in this patch. Because the lists are so similar, a single list is provided instead of the usual tabs. The lists are so close because Frost places a high value on Critical Strike, and there are not many great trinket options, regardless of build. As a rough guideline, follow the stat priority of Critical Strike > Mastery > Haste > Versatility.

        As always, the best way to determine what stats and gear you should use is to sim yourself

        Best Frost Death Knight Trinkets in The War Within Season 1

        Frost always wants an on-use trinket with a stat buff to pair with its cooldowns. After securing one, sims typically prefer the highest ilevel trinket for the other slot. Double on-use is a solid option, either with a damage on-use or stat on-use, but you can find passive options that perform just as well.

        S Tier
        • Mark of KhardrosExplanation
          Both on-use secondary stat trinkets available for Frost this season are nearly identical. Mark of Khardros gives slightly more Mastery when used over Skarmorak Shard. The 90-second cooldown makes this trinket for any build. It will line up with your cooldowns whether you play Deathbringer or Rider of the Apocalypse, Breath of Sindragosa or Obliteration.
        • Ara-Kara SacbroodExplanation
          Frost's strongest passive stat trinket this season, with a flavorful damage proc on top. Except to generally have a few stacks going at a time.
        • Skarmorak ShardExplanation
          Both on-use secondary stat trinkets available for Frost this season are nearly identical. Skarmorak Shard grants small Mastery buffs when you defeat enemies, making this a bit more attractive than Mark of Khardros in Mythic+. The 90-second cooldown makes this trinket for any build. It will line up with your cooldowns whether you play Deathbringer or Rider of the Apocalypse, Breath of Sindragosa or Obliteration.
        • Treacherous TransmitterExplanation
          A highly unique trinket that outpaces the competition when used properly. When you use it, you will get one of three tasks: stand on a plate, collect an orb, or jump three times. We want to complete the task right before using Pillar of Frost. The "stand here" and "catch the ball" tasks can be done by running to a spot a few steps forward and right from where you used the trinket. Learning this spot will be key to good timing on lining up the buff with Pillar of Frost. Consider this more strongly for Mythic+, where you can start the task before pulling a pack. Rider of the Apocalypse builds especially want this trinket work because the Strength buff will empower all the Horsemen. In contrast, the Mastery buff from the other on-use trinkets will do little for the Horsemen.
        A Tier
        • Ovi'nax's Mercurial EggExplanation
          It is difficult to gauge this trinket through sims because it revolves around movement. However, it has great potential. Our highest secondary stat will be Critical Strike, and we want it almost as much as we want Strength. It will not be a significant penalty if we move a lot and swap the Strength stacks for Critical Strike stacks. Suspending the Strength buff right before using Breath of Sindragosa will be ideal. However, there may not be time for the buff to reallocate fully if you move a lot.
        • Skyterror's Corrosive OrganExplanation
          A solid option for both Mythic+ and Raid. The splash range is short, so make sure enemies are gathered before using this trinket. The DoT applied by the trinket will intensify once per tick, so there is no need to force extra damage ticks while the trinket is active.
        B Tier
        • Spare Meat HookExplanation
          One of the better placeholders until you can get something better. A solid Strength stick with an okay damage Use effect.
        • Oppressive Orator's LarynxExplanation
          A better-than-average trinket for Mythic+. Nice passive Strength, and the on-use gives us some upfront burst going into a pack.
        • Mad Queen's MandateExplanation
          It is a good Critical Strike stick with interesting damage on use. After a brief channel, it deals a huge chunk of damage to your target in an attempt to execute it. This trinket may be fun to play with in Mythic+, where you'll have many opportunities to execute targets for more casts. Use this before or after Pillar of Frost, or you'll lose a GCD.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Frost Death Knight

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. WeaponsExplanation
          Current embellishment tuning puts a weapon as our first crafting priority to get the Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension embellishment. Before upgrading, ensure you have enough Crests to cover crafting a weapon with Enchanted Gilded Harbinger Crest. After crafting, prioritize upgrading your other weapon as much as possible. Weapons contribute more DPS per ilevel than any other slot.
        2. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Once you get a strong trinket like Ara-Kara Sacbrood, upgrading it will give you the most out of your limited currencies.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

        Crafting Notes

        Embellishments are relatively weak this season, with small gaps between the handful we will consider crafting. The important thing is to make sure you have two, even if you cannot have fully optimal embellishments for each slice of endgame. Prioritize a One-Hand weapon craft early. The power spike from a Mythic ilevel weapon at the start of the season cannot be matched by anything else. Two-Hand Frost is distinctly behind this season, so we can craft with a clear focus on one weapon. If you want to flex between Death Knight specs, target Boots and Cloak. These slots have the lowest stat budget, making them ideal for gear that caps out 3 ilevels below the maximum.

        Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

        Best Embellishments

        Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
        1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension Explanation
          Our best embellishment for all content. It provides a strong secondary stat buff, amplified by Writhing Armor Banding . Every 8 seconds in combat, the buff refreshes and switches to a new stat, resulting in roughly even uptime for each of the four secondary stats. It is harder to get total value from this embellishment in Mythic+ as it takes over a minute to ramp to full strength, and the timer is unforgiving if you drop combat in keys. Even in this worst-case scenario, it performs on par with other embellishments, making it our best overall pick.
        2. Writhing Armor Banding Explanation
          This embellishment doubles the value we get from Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension , allowing us to squeeze the most out of an embellishment that can only be applied to weapons. We are then free to put a 639 ilevel weapon in our main hand instead of using two crafted weapons capped at 636. Although the tooltip calls out a positive and negative effect on Nerubian embellishments, Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension only has a positive effect.
        3. Dawnthread Lining Explanation
          This is the next best option if you are concerned about dropping the buff from Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension . We craft it on the Wrist or Cloak slot because we want to craft on a piece with a low stat budget since crafted gear stops 3 item levels before fully upgraded Myth track gear. If you plan to play Unholy as well, craft with this embellishment. It has the best average performance between the specs and does not require you to sink Spark of Omens into something only one spec will use.
        4. Blessed Weapon Grip Explanation
          This weapon-only embellishment tends to sim lower than Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension ; however, it has no strings attached to its performance. A potential placeholder for the first week of dungeon farming when combat will be too short for Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension to stack up.
        5. Elemental Focusing Lens Explanation
          It generally falls behind other embellishments but is notable for being a decent pick for all three Death Knight specs. Someone who wants to craft for all three specs would look at something like one _JKL - Healer Damage Me and one Eruption
