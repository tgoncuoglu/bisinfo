    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Shadow Priest, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Shadow Priest in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Shadow Priest in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Shadow Priest gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Shadow Priest drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        As a rough guideline follow the stat priority of: Haste > Mastery >= Critical Strike > Versatility. Don't neglect the value of Intellect, a substantial increase in ilvl is generally worth it compared to optimized stats. It is always recommended that you use Raidbots to sim your own character since every character has different circumstances.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Shadow Priest

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Trinkets often vary in value and chase ones (such as Spymaster's Web) are often hard to obtain early on. It is best to sim your optimal upgrades using RaidBots, but high priority trinkets are always a strong first choice, depending on your Weapon situation.
        2. Main Hand WeaponExplanation
          This is your first priority to get high item level, but you do have an alternative option in crafting if you wish. If you get hold of a good enough option here, it may be better to do this and switch your Spark of Omens priority accordingly.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

        Best Crafted Gear for Shadow Priest

        Recommended Crafting Order

        2H vs 1H weapon is a bit of a question as to how quickly you can get a high ilvl MH / Weapon. For a lot of people the 2H with double spark and just committing to a 590 off piece like Cloak / Bracers with Writhing Armor Banding will be optimal for early season. Long term BiS is Sovereign's Disdain with Vagabond's Torch.
        1. Crafting a 2H Weapon is going to be the best option for most people, if you do this, do so with the Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension embellishment. Make sure this is an INTELLECT weapon.
        2. Craft Pioneer's Cloth Cuffs with Writhing Armor Banding if you crafted a 2h weapon and no longer have any more sparks, this can really go on a lot of different slots, it doesn't have to be wrists. Once you have a 3rd spark, upgrade this to Consecrated Cuffs or the equivalent on another slot.
        3. Alternatively if you get a high ilvl weapon early on you can craft items with Duskthread Lining on them.
        4. Any Low Item Level Piece.

        Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension is an embellishment that applies to weapons and provides a stacking buff over time. This is excellent for Raiding and Mythic+ alike and will be your go to embellishment option, paired with a Writhing Armor Banding to double its strength.
        The crafted ring Binding of Binding is a rather strong crafted option, but involves a unique mechanic where you have to pair to a specific ally "permanently" until you decide to pay the cost to change to a different player. There is potential for this to be strong much later down the line, especially for those doing Mythic+ dungeons with an organised group, but it is unlikely to be desired early on.
        Dawnthread Lining is a passive embellishment effect with a condition that is entirely reminiscent of Blue Silken Lining from Dragonflight. In this case, it can be reasonably expected that most players will maintain decent uptime of this effect by remaining above 80% health in most content, outside of certain dangerous moments in specific encounters.

        Critical Strike is not one of Shadow's better stats this expansion, but due to the weakness of embellishments as a whole it is still a good choice if you don't want to or can't use a crafted weapon embellishment.
        Duskthread Lining is a passive embellishment effect with a condition that is entirely reminiscent of Blue Silken Lining from Dragonflight. In this case, it can be reasonably expected that most players will maintain decent uptime of this effect by remaining above 80% health in most content, outside of certain dangerous moments in specific encounters.

        Versatility is not one of Shadow's better stats this expansion, but due to the weakness of embellishments as a whole it is still a good choice if you don't want to or can't use a crafted weapon embellishment.
        Darkmoon Sigil: Symbiosis is another option for a weapon embellishment for Shadow Priest, however it is notably behind other options.
        Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity is an interesting embellishment concept but falls flat due to Shadow only being able to cast spells from 1 spell school consistently. It is also poorly tuned.
        Blessed Weapon Grip is a rather straightforward effect that adapts to your stat priority by providing you a boost to your highest secondary stat. This effect isn't particularly powerful, and cannot be doubled up through the use of Writhing Armor Banding .
        Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

        While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Feverflare for Haste and Mastery. Visit the stats page for more information.

        Best Embellishments

        Embellishments are generally a LOT less powerful than they were in Dragonflight. Very few of them scale with ilvl and their average contribution is less than 1% per.
        • Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension - Is typically seen as our best Embellishment for both M+ and Raid. It does fall off in between M+ pulls, which you might see as bad, but Embellishments are so undertuned that it doesn't matter. You want this with Writhing Armor Banding .
        • Dawnthread Lining and Duskthread Lining are both fine, but generally slightly worse. Versatility is typically preferred due to the minor defensive benefit in this case.
        • Elemental Focusing Lens is another option if the uptime requirements of Dawnthread Lining and Duskthread Lining aren't to your liking and you do not have an embellished weapon.
        Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top.
