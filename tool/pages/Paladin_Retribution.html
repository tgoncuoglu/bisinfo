    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Retribution Paladin, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Retribution Paladin in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Retribution Paladin in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Retribution Paladin gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Retribution Paladin drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        In general, you'll want to prioritize getting as much Mastery as possible without dropping item level for it in most cases. If you're unsure, simming your gear options is generally the best way to figure out your optimal gear setup.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Retribution Paladin

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. WeaponExplanation
          Weapons have the largest contribution to your damage by far. You should always upgrade a weapon where possible before any other items.
        2. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Trinkets tend to have strong effects that are worth more than regular items at the same item level. Upgrading trinkets is usually going to be the next top priority.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          For the rest of your gear, you should aim to prioritize item slots that have higher stat bonuses like Helms and Chests.

        Best Crafted Gear for Retribution Paladin

        Recommended Crafting Order

        1. Any crafted items that you can use embellishments on
          • These can be your lowest item level slot initially for armor embellishments; you just want to make sure you can embellish them
        2. A Weapon
          • E.g. Everforged Greataxe
        3. Any low item level slots
          • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level.
          • It's best to simulate your character using the Droptimizer to pick out good upgrades.
        Two-handed weapons do cost 2 Spark of Omens rather than 1, which makes them a big investment, but they are the largest upgrade by a wide margin due to how important weapon DPS is. If you're desperate for a weapon early on it's a good strategy to craft one if you can craft it at 636, but you'll lose out on a second Spark of Omens item for a bit until you get your third Spark of Omens.

        Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

        While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Feverflare or Algari Missive of the Peerless to guarantee Mastery and either Haste or Critical Strike.

         Best Embellishments

          Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are fairly minor, so your decision on what to craft should generally be dictated by the slot that provides the biggest immediate upgrade first. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
          1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension with Writhing Armor Banding Explanation
            Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension is a weapon embellishment, which restricts you to needing to craft a weapon first and has a lower maximum item level than you could get from Mythic raiding or from the Vault. That said, the bonus is very strong when combined with Writhing Armor Banding , very similar to using two Dawnthread Lining depending on the average uptime, and you may want to craft a weapon early on anyway if you happen to get unlucky looting one from elsewhere. If you do, this is a strong embellishment combination to use, and Writhing Armor Banding will still provide full benefit if used on a lower item level non-Spark crafted item while you wait to acquire more Sparks.
          2. Dawnthread Lining Explanation
            This embellishment provides the largest bonus for armor embellishments, but it does require you to stay above 80% health to keep its bonus. If you find yourself having very low uptime on the buff then it's worth considering a swap to another embellishment.
          3. Elemental Focusing Lens Explanation
            This is the second best choice for armor embellishment. It's not a large damage proc, but it is still better than the alternatives.
          The theoretical best final slots to use armor embellishments on are Cloak and Wrists, since crafted gear has a lower item level cap and these slots provide less stats than most others. Early on you'll generally be happy to craft on any low item level slot, and swap them out later as needed.
