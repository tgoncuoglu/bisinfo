    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Survival Hunter, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Survival Hunter in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Survival Hunter in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Survival Hunter gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Survival Hunter drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        As a Survival Hunter, your BiS gear and stats to prioritize depend on the Hero talent you're playing: Pack Leader or Sentinel. You can see the rough priority of stats below as well as the BiS set of gear to work for. As always, it's a great idea to sim yourself!

        We are currently recommending Sentinel for all forms of PvE content as it is outperforming Pack Leader numerically. However, you will see recommendations for both below.

        Survival Hunters are mainly looking for one powerful on-use trinket to pair with their cooldowns, and a supplementary either proc or flat stat trinket.

        S Tier
        • Skardyn's GraceExplanation
          This is an immensely powerful trinket, lining up perfectly with the Relentless Primal Ferocity version of Coordinated Assault. The massive mastery bonus adds a tremendous amount of "oomph" onto our already fairly powerful cooldown, and works incredibly well for both Single Target and AoE damage situations.
        A Tier
        • Empowering Crystal of Anub'ikkajExplanation
          Although this is an entirely passive trinket with a little bit of randomness, it still provides a ton of secondary stats over time, making it a strong contender for both single target and AoE damage.
        • Void Reaper's ContractExplanation
          Performs very well for Single Target damage, but falls behind drastically for AoE. We cannot make use of the set effect since we cannot utilise the fist weapon that requires it.
        • Ara-Kara SacbroodExplanation
          Performs very well for both ST and AoE alike, making it an incredibly versatile trinket! it does not currently compare that well against some of the pre-season offerings, but those are unavailable at higher itemlevels once season 1 begins.
        • Dead-Eye SpyglassExplanation
          Very similar to Empowering Crystal of Anub'ikkaj, but has a downside attached to where in order to stack up the crit buff you need to be attacking the target it procced from. This can somewhat lower its expected stat returns in an environment where it can proc on a very fast dying mob you didn't neccesarily want it to proc onto. With that said, it still provides a great amount of critical strike.
        • Ovi'nax's Mercurial EggExplanation
          This is a bit of an awkward trinket, but on paper it provides a large amount of stats, which scales well into Single Target and AoE alike.
        B Tier
        • Mad Queen's MandateExplanation
          Can deal a respectable amount of burst damage, The execute effect is a nice touch in raids but its kill effect is annoying to play around in mythic+. and can conflict with other, more important on-use trinkets.
        • Quickwick CandlestickExplanation
          This can be a reasonable alternative to Skardyn's Grace in a pinch, however it is nowhere near as powerful even at 626, which is the maximum itemlevel of Quickwick Candlestick.
        • Sikran's Endless ArsenalExplanation
          This trinket has incredibly random but fun effects, although they aren't incredibly potent they do offer some bonus niche effects such as avoidance and run speed.
        • Bottled Flayedwing ToxinExplanation
          Deals very sustained single target damage, competing even with some of the S-tier options. However, it struggles to deal AoE damage effectively, dropping its overall rating down a lot.
        • Harvester's EdictExplanation
          Incredibly awkard, but very powerful in theory. A lot of the value here comes from the ability to intercept the orb. Whether this is easily doable in melee remains to be seen, If not, the true rating is likely closer to C tier.
        • Imperfect Ascendancy SerumExplanation
          Since we are very much in the market for a 2-minute on use trinket, we can utilise this. However, it is not particularly powerful or worth aiming for. its maximum itemlevel also stops it from being truly competitive. However, if you randomly end up receiving this trinket it might be okay for a bit.
        • Treacherous TransmitterExplanation
          A fairly versatile and strong trinket, but the tasks can be a little tedious or restrictive, requiring you to chase an orb, jump a few times, or to stand in a purple zone. These tasks are especially restrictive in Mythic+, where the trinket performs best.
        C Tier
        • Twin Fang InstrumentsExplanation
          Can deal a respectable amount of burst damage, but its use effect awkwardly conflicts with that of other on-use trinkets.
        • Mistcaller OcarinaExplanation
          A somewhat interesting trinket this tier given that the trinket power across the board for this first season is fairly low. The true performance scales based on the content you are doing, given that it ideally requires you to buff 5 DPS characters for optimal performance. With that in mind, it is D tier for solo content, B tier for Mythic+, and A tier for Raid content.

          You can use Mistcaller's Ballad or its other stat variants to change the stat the trinket grants by purchasing them from Master Clerk Salorn.
        D Tier
        • Overclocked Gear-a-Rang LauncherExplanation
          The lack of any primary or secondary stat hurts this trinket in particular. The random procced damage it deals is nowhere near worth enough to give up the raw stats.
        Other Trinkets
        • Concoction: Kiss of DeathExplanation
          Quite interesting, although nothing about this trinket is favourable. its cooldown does not line up, its effect does not perfectly line up, it has the potential to stun you if you forget about deactivating it, and its maximum itemlevel is limited to 626.
        • Spelunker's Waning CandleExplanation
          Another delve trinket, aiming more at potentionally buffing allies. However, unlike Mistcaller Ocarina it appears to split the buff with your ally rather than getting the full effect. This means you might not want to share the buff at all if you happen to have your cooldowns active! As with all Delve trinkets, the maximum itemlevel is limited at 626.
        • Everburning LanternExplanation
          This is technically a ranged trinket, but we can proc it through some attachs such as Symphonic Arsenal and Wildfire Bomb. With that said, it still performs poorly.
        • Scroll of MomentumExplanation
          Possibly interesting. it is currently unclear how much the damage effect scales with speed effects. However, given how far down its performance currently is i doubt that even Aspect of the Cheetah could make up for it.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Survival Hunters

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Trinkets such as  Skardyn's Grace are incredibly potent, and raising their item level results in a relatively larger power boost compared to upgrading pieces that don't carry as much stats, such as wrists or cloaks.
        2. Weapon (if not crafting)Explanation
          Similar to trinkets, Weapons affect your DPS a disproportionate amount relative to other pieces. Although we're ideally looking to get a Harvester's Interdiction, upgrading something else such as a Black Shepherd's Guisarme or Honored Executioner's Perforator (which would enable the discounted upgrades for the former) can also be worth it if you are not expecting to get a mythic weapon any time soon.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims. Because of the way discounts work which can remove the cost of Runed Harbinger Crest entirely for any item you own up until the highest itemlevel in that slot. If you wish to truly optimise the Runed Harbinger Crest it is advised to attempt to get an item in that slot from mythic+ first before spending an extra stage worth of upgrades costing you extra Runed Harbinger Crest.

          For instance, if you manage to get yourself a 610 hero-track Harvester's Interdiction from a +7, obtaining a 613  Endoskeletal Polecrank or Black Shepherd's Guisarme from a +10 later down the line will allow you to upgrade the Harvester's Interdiction without costing you any extra Runed Harbinger Crest.
          Ofcourse, this degree of optimisation is not necessary, but it can help you squeeze those extra few itemlevels out of your crests.

        Best Crafted Gear for Survival Hunter

        Recommended Crafting Order

        1. Charged Halberd| Everforged Greataxe| Vagabond's Careful Crutch with Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension attached.
          • While a weapon does set you back two Spark of Omens, it ultimately does equal a max itemlevel Harvester's Interdiction even if you consider committing the extra embellishment, while also not having you deal with any of the possible RNG of obtaining a powerful weapon.
          • If by any chance a good weapon such as Harvester's Interdiction appears in your vault before you obtain 2 Spark of Omens, you can consider other options instead to maximise itemlevel.
        2. A Crafted Piece (e.g. Consecrated Cloak)
          • Use Writhing Armor Banding on this piece.
        3. Any Low Item Level Piece
          • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level.
          • It's best to simulate your character using the Droptimizer to pick out good upgrades.

        Consecrated Cloak - It may seem a bit unintuitive to craft a cloak because the cloak slot carries a low stat budget, but there is a good reason for this.

        First, crafted items "only" go to 636 Itemlevel, meaning that on their own they don't quite compete with a maximized 639 itemlevel item in the long run. in this scenario you'd ideally want your embellishments to go towards your low-budget slots such as cloaks.

        Second, a cloak is a slot that is not capable of rolling with RNG-sockets from sources such as mythic plus. If you were to craft yourself a Glyph-Etched Vambraces instead, you might deny yourself a lucky socket proc from a bracer drop elsewhere.
        Charged Halberd| Everforged Greataxe| Vagabond's Careful Crutch - These are all effectively the same item, so you can pick whichever is the cheapest for you to craft. The primary reason to even craft this in the first place is to pair it with a Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension . If this is something you are not planning to do, then crafting a weapon might not be a good idea.

        Note: Although all of the listed weapons perform identical, crafting anything other than the polearm on a polearm-themed spec may or may not be considered uncool.
        Glyph-Etched Breastplate| Glyph-Etched Guise| Glyph-Etched Epaulets| Glyph-Etched Cuisses| Glyph-Etched Gauntlets - Although any of these listed pieces carry a massive stat budget and can in turn be massive upgrades, these pieces conflict with the item slots required to match our 4-piece Lightless Scavenger's Necessities set bonus. It is advised to hold off on crafting anything onto these slots until you have acquired your 4-piece set bonus.
        Vambraces of Deepening Darkness - This can technically be BiS as far as single target output is concerned, however it only jumps ahead by about 0.8% once you pair it with Writhing Armor Banding . This will also double the slow effect raising it to a whopping 30%. While it is not a permanent slow, the strength and random nature of this slow makes it much easier to be caught out of an unrecoverable position than some similar items we may have used in the past, such as Acidic Hailstone Treads. Furthermore, with the loss of the extra 3 yard range on Lunge we are much less capable of "premoving" and in general being at a slightly safer distance than we used to be. With all that in mind, we actively recommend not to craft these.

        Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

        While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Peerless or a Algari Missive of the Feverflare to guarantee Mastery, and haste or crit, unless the piece has fixed stats already present or your stat balance leans you toward a different recommendation.

        Best Embellishments

        Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top, your decision on what to craft should (generally) be dictated by the slot rather than these effects with some small exceptions. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
        1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension + Writhing Armor Banding Explanation
          Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension , like the Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity can only be attached to crafted weaponry. However, unlike Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity it counts as a Nerubian Embellished item, allowing the Writhing Armor Banding to boost its effects. It provides a nice amount of stats and while a bit random, does make up for it in volume.
        2. Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity Explanation
          Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity can only be attached to crafted weaponry, but is a very powerful embellishment. Because we wield 2-handed weapons, we can only use one of these.
        3. Dawnthread Lining Explanation
          While not incredibly powerful, it provides consistent benefit. although it isn't always active, provided you can keep the buff active ~40% of the time or more, it will beat other competing options.
        4. Duskthread Lining Explanation
          This is simply an alternative to Dawnthread Lining . Versatility is slightly weaker for us than crit, but it may be favourable for M+ situations where this embellishment can very slightly raise your maximum effective health through the defensive benefits of versatility.

          These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant adverts on other sites.
