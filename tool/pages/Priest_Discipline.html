    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Discipline Priest, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Discipline Priest in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Discipline Priest in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Discipline Priest gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Discipline Priest drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        As a rough guideline for raid preparation, the stat priority for Raiding Discipline Priest is: Haste > Critical Strike >= Mastery > Versatility . Don't neglect the value of Intellect, a substantial increase in ilvl is generally worth it compared to optimized stats. It is always recommended that you use Questionably Epic Live to sim your own character, since every character is different.

        The following lists below will prioritize gear for raid, whereas a Mythic+ priority would involve different secondaries in your priority, specifically Versatility in more slots. You will still prioritize higher item level overall.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Discipline Priest

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Trinkets that you are unlikely to replace are always excellent choices for upgrading.
        2. Main Hand WeaponExplanation
          This is your first priority to get high item level, but you do have an alternative option in crafting if you wish. If you get hold of a good enough option here, it may be better to do this and switch your Spark of Omens priority accordingly.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

        Best Crafted Gear for Discipline Priest

        Recommended Crafting Order

        Do not craft anything except blues yet! They are still making changes every single day. Check back before season 1 launches for the latest information.

        Your first craft is an important choice but it's also a slightly convoluted one. Here are your options:
        1. A crafted two handed weapon with Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension on it.
          • I will clear Mythic bosses or M+9s on second week of the season: Craft nothing week 1. Craft your staff at its maximum (636) item level on week 2 with Gilded Crests. This is the highest possible value for your crest spend. The downside is that it leaves you without a craft for the upcoming Heroic / M0 week. It might also not be a viable path if you're not interested in going into Mythic raid or Mythic+9s right away.
          • I am a heroic raider or do medium keys: If you are not going to have any Gilded crests on week two of the season then it's ok to craft a weapon using your heroic crests instead.
          • Note that Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension is much worse in Mythic+ where stacks fall off regularly. It's still a good early craft because you want the big weapon item level anyway but you might transition to something like Duskthread Lining a month or two into the season.
          • Be very careful that you do not craft agility weapons. They do not work.
        2. Craft Writhing Armor Banding on a low budget slot like bracers or cape. This will double the power of your weapon embellishment. Eventually you'll want this on a higher item level piece but for week 1 it's ok to craft this at 590 item level.
        3. Any Low Item Level Piece
          • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level.
          • It's best to simulate your character using the QE Live Upgrade Finder to pick out good upgrades.
        It's also worth noting that if at any point you're really struggling to pick up a main-hand weapon and you have a spare spark, that will always be the strongest immediate upgrade available.

        Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

        While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Fireflash to guarantee Haste and Crit, unless the piece has fixed stats already present or your stat balance leans you toward a different recommendation.

        Best Embellishments

        More updates coming to this section soon, hold off on crafting until then!

        Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top, your decision on what to craft should (generally) be dictated by the slot rather than these effects with some small exceptions. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
        1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension *Explanation
          A very solid choice that offers a lot of overall stats. You do need to keep a fairly continuous combat going to keep your stacks maxed so this should be consistent in raid but fairly annoying to play in Mythic+.
        2. Writhing Armor Banding *Explanation
          This embellish duplicates the weapon embellish, making it the preferred second embellish for raid performance.
        3. Duskthread Lining *Explanation
          While we don't love effects that drop off when we drop low on healers, we should be above 80% enough of the time to get some value from this - particularly in Mythic+ where Versatility is also even more useful.
