    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Vengeance Demon Hunter, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Vengeance Demon Hunter in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Vengeance Demon Hunter in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Vengeance Demon Hunter gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Vengeance Demon Hunter drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        Vengeance Demon Hunter gearing is much simpler than one might expect. You can follow a general rule of taking the highest item level, and then only comparing secondary stats when two pieces of gear have the same item level. As a rough guideline, after taking item level into account, defensively you should follow the stat priority of: Item Level >>>> Haste = Critical Strike > Versatility > Mastery. It is always recommended that you use Raidbots to sim your own character offensively, since every character is different.

        Overall Vengeance Demon Hunter Best in Slot Gear

        This table does not contain the absolute best in all scenarios, but if you're looking for a simple list to aim for, this will make it easier. Trinkets, in particular, are dependent on the situation, so you can check out the Cheat Sheet or the below Trinket section for an easy breakdown, but some of the decent ones have been included here. Unfortunately this expansion, most flat damage trinkets have a 33% penalty for tank specializations making them somewhat less competitive than stat-based ones.

        Best in Slot Gear from Mythic+ for Vengeance Demon Hunter

There are several other options for each slot, so don't feel like you need to farm for these specific items, particularly trinkets since they are situational.         Best Vengeance Demon Hunter Trinkets in The War Within Season 1

        Vengeance Demon Hunters are looking for good on-use trinkets defensively, or with a strong proc. Secondary stats tend to be pretty good, but a strong proc can still outweigh raw damage despite a 33% penalty to tank specs. Offensively, whatever sims highest is best. This is an evaluation of all trinkets that are available from common content, excluding limited-time items. As Vengeance, trinkets from older content are generally not worth using, nor anything that isn't available at maximum item level, unless it is powerful enough to overcome the deficit.

        You can click on the notes icons next to each item for a rundown on the reasoning!

        A+ Tier
        • Bottled Flayedwing ToxinExplanation
          For unknown reasons, this item does not have the 33% penalty for non-DPS specs while having static Agility, so it deals more damage than any other trinket available. When you use it, it grants an hour-long buff that allows your attacks to proc a Nature DoT Flayedwing Toxin with 4+ Haste RPPM.
        • Ara-Kara SacbroodExplanation
          This trinket provides static Haste and a 2.5 RPPM effect of this trinket which grants an Agility buff for a entire minute before hatching, dealing a Nature DoT to an enemy. Each egg proc generates a separate buff, so once it's running, it grants on average 2.5x as much Agility as stated on the tooltip.
        • Foul Behemoth's CheliceraExplanation
          This is classed as a tank trinket, so it doesn't have the damage penalty of DPS trinkets. Because of this, it does damage that is nearly on par with full DPS trinkets, while also having defensive benefits! This is exceptionally good in Mythic+ because it has a short 90 second cooldown, while granting a max health buff that's great for bosses, while generating a shield in between pulls that helps buffer us during our weakest point - on pull before our defensives get rolling. It grants static Agility on top of the buff that can stack up to 20 times.
        A Tier
        • Empowering Crystal of Anub'ikkajExplanation
          While not particularly powerful at first glance, this is one of the strongest secondary stat trinkets available, and Vengeance benefits quite a lot from stats. It has static Agility and has 1.55 RPPM on a 20 second buff, so on average it should have ~51.7% uptime.
        • Void Reaper's ContractExplanation
          Despite the 33% penalty to non-tanks, this is still one of our best offensive trinkets, especially when the set bonus is active from using Void Reaper's Warp Blade.
        • Dead-Eye SpyglassExplanation
          This trinket grants static Agility and a massive amount of Crit with 1 RPPM for 10 seconds with roughly 16.7% uptime.
        • Sikran's Endless ArsenalExplanation
          This trinket grants static Agility and a 60 second cd on-use which rotates between three different on-use effects and a minor passive effect that lasts until the trinket is used again:
          • Surekian Flourish - Apply a Shadow DoT to your current single target; passive Parry and Avoidance.
          • Stance - Surekian Decimation - Physical line frontal with split damage; passive 10% additional critical strike damage to absorb shields.
          • Surekian Barrage - Shadow damage split on all targets 20 yards in front of you; passive stacking movement speed buff while standing still up to +30%, and moving activates it for 6 seconds.
          This has the 33% penalty to non-dps, but is still one of the strongest offensive trinkets. The passive Parry is quite good in Mythic+ as well, but using two on-use trinkets can be clunky, and it is only up 33% of the time.
        B Tier
        • Twin Fang InstrumentsExplanation
          This trinket has the 33% non-DPS spec penalty. It grants static Agility and has an on-use ability with a 2 minute cooldown. After using it, it can be used twice more for a total of 3 times within 20 seconds before it goes on cooldown. The way it works is as follows:
          1. First Cast: activates Nx's Shadow Strike, dealing an 8 yard frontal cone of Shadow damage that is fully uncapped and has linear scaling.
          2. Second Cast: activates Vx's Frost Slash, dealing a 30 yard frontal cone of Frost damage for twice the amount of Nx's Shadow Strike, split amongst all enemies hit.
          3. Third Cast: activates both the prior casts at the same time.
        • Mad Queen's MandateExplanation
          This trinket grants static Crit and an on-use with a 2 minute cooldown. It has a 1 second cast time, during which you are unable to dodge or parry, but deals a massive amount of damage over 2 sec to a Priority target, and it deals scaling increased damage to enemies with missing health. In addition, if the enemy dies during the debuff, the cooldown is cut in half to 1 minute, making it a fantastic option in multi-target fights. However, by default it's not as strong as other trinkets that aren't reliant on the cooldown reduction.
        • Silken Chain WeaverExplanation
          This trinket is a purely defensive option in M+, with a 15% Attack speed reduction for 15 seconds on an extremely short 1 minute cooldown, while also granting a small shield. The shield isn't particularly good, but 25% uptime on the largest source of damage intake that is available for every pull is fantastic. Unfortunately, coming from Delves, it has a maximum item level of 626.
        Other Trinkets
        • Throngus's FingerExplanation
          Despite granting static Vers, this trinket grants a 20 sec dodge buff once per minute, which can be a large amount of damage reduction over the course of a Mythic+ dungeon due to the melees avoided. It has roughly 33% uptime.
        • Ovi'nax's Mercurial EggExplanation
          This trinket has no baseline stats. Instead you generate stacks of either Agility or your highest secondary stat based on whether you are standing still or moving, at a rate of 1 per second. When you activate the on-use, it will freeze the stack counts at their current amounts for 20 sec once every 2 minutes. This is pretty annoying to use, so despite the potentially large gain, it loses some value.
        • Scroll of MomentumExplanation
          This is technically a DPS trinket, so it suffers from the 33% damage penalty to non-tanks, and since it comes from Delves, its maximum item level is only 626. Despite this, because the damage based on movement speed increase portion applies to all increases, including those from our talents, this trinket is actually still decent.
        • Ceaseless SwarmglandExplanation
          This trinket has static Agility and a 6+Haste RPPM proc which occurs on damage taken, putting a 6 second DoT on enemies that also reduces their damage dealt to you by a flat amount for 6 seconds. It's not bad overall, but not great.
        • Charming CourierExplanation
          This trinket has static Haste and 3+ Haste RPPM proc to summon a pet that deals damage and grants a 50% absorb shield. In addition, it has a 1 minute cooldown on-use which summons 12+Haste scarabs over 3 seconds that each do the same as the random procs. Effectively, it's equivalent to 15+Haste procs per minute, which is decent but not super strong.
        • Abyssal TrapExplanation
          Grants static Agility and an on-use with 1 minute cd that deals uncapped AoE damage. It's pretty solid but has a maximum item level of 626.
        • Algari Alchemist StoneExplanation
          Grants static Versatility and a 1 RPPM proc of a large Agility buff as well as boosting the effect of healing potions. Not great, but a decent crafted option if you have spare sparks and no good trinkets. It has a maximum item level of 636.
        • Treacherous TransmitterExplanation
          This trinket has static Haste and an on-use that grants a massive amount of Agility every 90 seconds. However, it is held back by the fact that you must execute one of 3 instructions for it:
          • Chase a Purple orb around
          • Stand in a purple disc projector
          • Jump 3 times
          The first two are extremely detrimental to positioning as a tank due to mechanics, and the third is annoying for Demon Hunters, since we can't spam jump due to Double Jump not triggering it.
        • Refracting Aggression ModuleExplanation
          This trinket has static Agility and whenever you taunt, it grants a shield that reduces damage you take by 50%, up to a cap with a 1 minute ICD. Unfortunately, the amount of shield is extremely low despite being on-demand, and incentivizes taunting for purposes other than regaining agro.
        • Overclocked Gear-a-Rang LauncherExplanation
          This trinket has no stats, but instead has an on-use with 2 min cd that deals damage to enemies in an arc. This damage is reduced for each enemy hit. In addition, it adds a small passive proc on auto-attacks which can reduce the on-use cooldown by 5 sec and adds a small amount of Fire damage to your next melee. It's not great.
        • Harvester's EdictExplanation
          This trinket has static Agility and a 1.66 RPPM proc on dealing damage which spawns an orb flying to your current target, dealing split AoE damage. Intercepting the orb grants Mastery for 15 seconds instead. Due to the 33% non-DPS spec penalty, we always want to grab the orb, but it's not great for damage due to being Mastery.
        • Skardyn's GraceExplanation
          This trinket grants static Agility and a huge mastery buff for 20 seconds on a 2 minute cooldown. Sadly, mastery is not very good.
        • Mistcaller OcarinaExplanation
          This trinket grants static Agility and when used, grants a 15 minute buff on you that has a 1 RPPM proc to grant allies a selected secondary stat for 15 seconds. It's pretty bad despite being a group support item.
        • Delve TrinketsExplanation
          All the remaining Delve trinkets are rather lackluster and don't have particularly good effects while also being 13 item levels lower.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Vengeance Demon Hunter

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. WeaponsExplanation
          Your mainhand weapon is the largest damage upgrade so that should be the first upgrade, no matter what. If you don't have access to a strong weapon early, since one of our strongest embellishments is Blessed Weapon Grip on an Everforged Longsword, you can spend crests on that first, then swap to a Mythic weapon in mainhand later.
        2. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Trinkets are generally the next largest power gain, so you'll want to upgrade any good trinkets you find after your weapon(s), whichever you happen to get first.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

        Best Crafted Gear for Vengeance Demon Hunter

        Recommended Crafting Order

          1. Everforged Longsword (with Blessed Weapon Grip )
            • The weapon embellishment is likely one of our most powerful embellishments since it grants our highest secondary.
            • Crafting a weapon early is generally the largest possible upgrade, since getting a max Mythic item level weapon of 639 is fairly unlikely. Additionally, we can swap it to our offhand later for minimal loss.
          2. Adrenal Surge Clasp or your lowest slot of Consecrated Cloak/ Rune-Branded Armbands/ Rune-Branded Kickers/ Rune-Branded Waistband with Pouch of Pocket Grenades
            • The belt is our second best embellishment in AoE since it procs off taking damage and consistently grants mainstat. The mastery loss is inconsequential since it is our worst secondary stat anyway. Unfortunately, it's not particularly good in ST.
            • Alternatively, for raw ST damage, upgrading your lowest slot with Pouch of Pocket Grenades on it will provide the most value.
          3. Any Low Item Level Piece
            • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level. In particular, accessory slots such as your neck or rings are an excellent choice due to coming pre-socketed with the exact secondary stats you desire.
            • It's best to simulate your character using the Droptimizer to pick out good upgrades.

          Crafting Notes

            Waders of the Unifying Flame
          Adrenal Surge Clasp - While this doesn't have our ideal stat combination of Crit and Haste on it, Crit and Vers is a good second option. It also grants mainstat, which is uncommon amongst embellishments while only costing our least favorite secondary of Mastery when it procs. If you choose not to use the crafted weapon, you can also double its power with Writhing Armor Banding on a low-budget slot. The proc rate is 2 RPPM with a 12 second duration and 15 second ICD, so it's up roughly 40% of the time.
          This is a set bonus that requires you to use two of three embellished items - Roiling Thunderstrike Talons/ Weathered Stormfront Vest/ Rook Feather Wristwraps. The bonus grants Fury of the Stormrook, causing lightning to occasionally strike nearby, spawning an orb for you to collect that grants a short haste buff. Unfortunately, the secondaries on the items are not ideal, and they overlap with our Tier slots, which would force us to use suboptimal pieces.
          Waders of the Unifying Flame - These boots grant a large amount of secondary stats from healing allies and standing on top of them, which isn't possible on a Demon Hunter so it's not something to consider.

          Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

          While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Fireflash to guarantee Crit and Haste, unless the piece has fixed stats already present or your stat balance leans you toward a different recommendation.

          Best Embellishments

            Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top, your decision on what to craft should (generally) be dictated by the slot rather than these effects with some small exceptions. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
            1. Blessed Weapon Grip Explanation
              This embellishment grants 10 stacks of your highest secondary on a 1RPPM proc, with the stacks diminishing every 3 seconds over the next 30 seconds. This averages out to roughly 50% uptime on half the stacks, which is our strongest AoE embellishment and one of the top overall.
            2. Adrenal Surge ClaspExplanation
              This belt comes with an embellishment and default stats of Crit and Vers. The 2 RPPM effect grants Agility when taking damage, while reducing Mastery slightly for 12 seconds, so it's a very strong option since tanks are generally always taking damage.
            3. Pouch of Pocket Grenades Explanation
              This is an alternative option for the belt on any crafted armor piece, which will grant raw damage. It's a good option if you want to use a low budget armor slot, since crafted items are 3 item levels lower, and it deals good ST damage.
            4. Writhing Armor Banding Explanation
              This is an alternative option to pair with the belt on any crafted armor piece, which will double its effect. It's a good option if you want to use a low budget armor slot, since crafted items are 3 item levels lower.

              The Wowhead Client is a little application we use to keep our database up to date, and to provide you with some nifty extra functionality on the website!  

              It serves 2 main purposes:  

              1. It maintains a WoW addon called the Wowhead Looter, which collects data as you play the game!  
              2. It uploads the collected data to Wowhead in order to keep the database up-to-date!  

              You can also use it to keep track of your completed quests, recipes, mounts, companion pets, and titles! 

              So, what are you waiting for? Download the client and get started.
