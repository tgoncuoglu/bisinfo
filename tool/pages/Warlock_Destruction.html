      Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Destruction Warlock, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

      In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Destruction Warlock in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

      Recommended Gear for Destruction Warlock in The War Within

      What is the BiS Destruction Warlock gear?

      With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Destruction Warlock drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
          As a general guideline follow the stat priority of: Haste > Mastery = Critical Strike > Versatility. Don't neglect the value of Intellect, a substantial increase in ilvl is typically going to be worth it compared to optimized stats. It is always recommended that you use Raidbots to sim your own character, since every character is different.

          Best Items to Upgrade for Destruction Warlock

          Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
          1. Strong TrinketsExplanation
            Trinkets often vary in value and chase ones (such as Spymaster's Web) are often hard to obtain early on. It is best to sim your optimal upgrades using RaidBots, but high priority trinkets are always a strong first choice, depending on your Weapon situation.
          2. Main Hand WeaponExplanation
            This is your first priority to get high item level, but you do have an alternative option in crafting if you wish. If you get hold of a good enough option here, it may be better to do this and switch your Spark of Omens priority accordingly.
          3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
            Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

          Best Crafted Gear for Destruction Warlock

          Recommended Crafting Order

          1. A Crafted Weapon (e.g. Vagabond's Bounding Baton/ Vagabond's Torch w/ Mastery and Critical Strike)
            • Use Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension on these item. You can only apply Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension to weapons.
            • It is VERY important to note that crafting a Staff costs 2 Spark of Omens, while a Main Hand or Off-Hand costs Spark of Omens. If you are able to loot either a MH or OH during the first week OR your Geat Vault of Mythic week, its advised to craft a MH/OH for 1 spark, and a second piece with your other Spark. Waiting until the first Great Vault of the Season to craft is HIGHLY recommended, but first crafts choices will vary widely.
          2. Any Low Item Level Piece
            • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens on an item with Writhing Armor Banding as the Embellishment.
            • It is also possible to wear a 590 Crafted item of lesser value (such as Bracers or Cloak, which do not require a Spark of Omens) with Writhing Armor Banding Embellished, and craft a different piece of gear with you next spark that is of greater value.
            • It's best to simulate your character using the Droptimizer to pick out good upgrades.
          It's also worth noting that if at any point you're really struggling to pick up a main-hand weapon and you have a spare spark, that will always be the strongest immediate upgrade available.

          Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful Dragonflight equipment, so you should spend them wisely aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note as well that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Similarly, you can use the same recrafting system to change your Season 3 Spark of Dreams crafted items to Season 4, bringing them forward with the new Enchanted Aspect's Awakened Crest. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

          While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity.

          Best Embellishments

          Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top, your decision on what to craft should (generally) be dictated by the slot rather than these effects with some small exceptions. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
          1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension Explanation
            This Embellishment grants a random secondary stat effect that stack in combat every 8 seconds. If you leave combat, the effect will drop, but will begin gaining stacks once re-entering combat. The effect is universally applicable in Mythic + and Raiding alike, making it a great overall choice.
          2. Writhing Armor Banding Explanation
            This embellishment doubles the effect of other Nerubian Embellishments, which Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension is! The effect is universally applicable in Mythic + and Raiding alike, making it a great overall choice.
          3. Elemental Focusing Lens *Explanation
            This Embellishment provides a damage based effect which deals damage based on the types of gems you have chosen for your gear. While it is technically our strongest single target embellishment, it has very little 2+ target value.
          4. Dawnthread Lining  Explanation
            Similar to Blue Silken Lining on Dragonflight, this Embellishment provides additional Critical Strike if you are above 80% health. The uptime of this effect will vary based on the encounter at hand, making it better/worse at times.
