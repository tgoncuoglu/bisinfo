    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Mistweaver Monk, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Mistweaver Monk in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

     Recommended Gear for Mistweaver Monk in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Mistweaver Monk gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Mistweaver Monk drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        Mistweaver Monks have a roughly similar priority between raid and mythic+ encounters, where we want to follow Intellect >> Haste > Critical Strike = Versatility > Mastery for raids and Intellect >> Haste > Crit > Mastery >= Versatility in Mythic+.

        Intellect, and thus item level as a whole, is what we want to prioritize first and foremost, as a means of increasing the base power of all of our Spells and Abilities. When we get to the point where we have multiple items available to us—with different secondary stat combinations—we want to gather as much Haste as possible, to increase the healing of our Haste-affected spells, like Renewing Mist and Enveloping Mist, and to lower the cooldown of Haste-affected spells, like Rising Sun Kick.

        After that, Critical Strike and Versatility are very close to each other in raids, as Versatility is the only stat that increases the healing of several spells. Mastery falls a little bit behind in the Rising Mist  Jade Bond  Invoke Yu'lon, the Jade Serpent build. Even though we're generating plenty of Gust of Mists events for her cooldown reduction, the rest of our healing is just too much greater than the healing Mastery contributes.

         Best Mistweaver Monk Trinkets in The War Within Season 1

        Mistweavers, like all healers in general, are looking for dependable, consistent trinkets that we can either use frequently to supplement our healing, or ones whose effects are generally always useful. Having two trinkets with activated effects equipped has one incur a 30-second cooldown whenever the other is used, which prevents some potentially egregious pairings of trinkets, but as healers we're able to space their uses out, so that generally isn't a concern for us. Most of the time, we want to pair our activated trinkets with an upcoming cooldown, so having trinkets that match our cooldown timers makes them all the more appealing.

        S Tier
        • Gale of ShadowsExplanation
          Providing a base amount of Haste and a stacking Intellect buff that doesn't decay or have any gimmicks associated with it, the only prerequisite being that you need to have periodic effects active, is fantastic for us. Renewing Mist and Enveloping Mist will ensure we stay at the maximum amount of stacks at all times, making this the best trinket possible for Mistweaver Monks.
        A Tier
        • Ara-Kara SacbroodExplanation
          With a baseline amount of Haste (do you see the pattern starting to form?), the passive effect provides a surprisingly good amount of Intellect, making it just a hair stronger than [item=178708 bonus=[=gv-sl-dun:6917] and its effect. The damage that it does is just a nice extra perk this trinket brings, but it isn't the reason it ranks as high as it does.
        • Unbound ChangelingExplanation
          With this trinket set to Haste, it's one of the best options we could hope for in raids. Similar to Gale of Shadows, this is just a great passive trinket that increases our Intellect and Haste.
        • Empowering Crystal of Anub'ikkajExplanation
          This is just a nice stat stick! While it can give us Haste, Critical Strike, or Versatility, it's lowered a few positions because of the possibility of it giving us Mastery. In Mythic+ however, its ranking is much more impressive because of this! Not much else to say about it though, the base amount of Intellect is great, and the secondary stat buff lasts long enough to make great use of it.
        • Creeping CoagulumExplanation
          When you use this trinket, it reduces your healing by 20% until you meet its cap, which happens quickly, and then it turns that amplifies that stored healing to heal several allies. Thankfully, there's an additional effect if it overheals its targets, allowing them to deal some extra damage to their target. This trinket is definitely one that you need to time its use very well with upcoming mechanics, but at least there's still some value if you get the timing a little off, or it decides to heal someone who doesn't need the healing. Because you can guarantee the five allies it'll healin Mythic+, this is defintiely something worth keeping if you haven't had much luck getting better trinkets.
        B Tier
        • Ovi'nax's Mercurial EggExplanation
          This trinket allows you to decide how much Intellect or Haste it gives you, with Intellct's stacks climbing while you stay still and diminishing while moving, while Haste works vice versa. There's a cooldown that allows you to keep your current buff numbers frozen for 20 seconds, but outside of this, you want to try and maintain 20 stacks of Intellect and 10 stacks of Haste. This trinket requires a fair bit of work to optimize, and there are other trinkets that do what it does without needing to track the stacks of two buffs.
        Other Trinkets
        • Anything in C Tier or below is just not worth consideration for Mistweavers. There are some options that are decent as you start gearing your character, but you'll want to replace them as soon as you can.
        • Spymaster's Web is one of the best trinkets for other healers, as of current tuning, but is missing from our list. This is because there needs to be damaging spells being cast every 6 seconds, and Mistweaver just...doesn't have those. All of our damaging abilities are flagged as Abilities, meaning they don't interact with this or any other trinkets that require damaging spells to trigger their effects. In the past, we had the tools available to still activate trinkets like this, like Eye of the Tiger for Tome of Unstable Power, but with the revamp of our Monk Class Talent Tree in The War Within, it has been removed. This trinket is just not usable for Mistweavers.

         Best Items to Upgrade for Mistweaver Monks

        Completing some activities at max level in The War Within will net you both Harbinger Crests as well as Valorstones, which you can use to upgrade items you acquire to their full potential, explained here. Because the Harbinger Crests are limited in how many you can acquire each week, there's a bit of a difficult decision to make at the start of the season. Once you start getting pieces of gear at the highest upgrade path you're comfortable achieving, there's a bit of a baseline priority you can follow, laid out below:
        1. Your Main Hand WeaponExplanation
          For specializations that use Intellect, a sizable percent of it is stored on your Weapon, whether it be a two-handed staff or a one-handed axe or mace or anything. If you manage to acquire one early on that's on a high upgrade path, it will provide the highest throughput increase over any other item you could upgrade.
        2. TrinketsExplanation
          If you manage to acquire a powerful trinket, one that you're going to have equipped for potentially the rest of the season, you'll want to upgrade that as soon as you can, to keep your healing as strong as possible.
        3. Other Gear / Low Item Level PiecesExplanation
          Beyond your weapon and trinkets, you'll want to upgrade your most powerful pieces of gear, typically gear that goes in the Head, Chest, or Leg slots. And while these slots have some of the highest Intellect and secondary stat rating budgets, once you make them as strong as you can, you can't forget about the rest of your gear.

         Best Crafted Gear for Mistweaver Monk

        Recommended Order for Crafted Gear

        1. Vagabond's Bounding Baton with Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension added to it.
          • If you will be going into Mythic raiding or taking on high (9+ Keystone Level) Mythic+ dungeons the first week they're available, do not craft anything until those difficulties are available. Because of the limited rate you can acquire Gilded Harbinger Crest and Runed Harbinger Crest, you want to use as few as possible, so recrafting it from the previous week will greatly limit how muh you'll be able to upgrade your other slots of gear.
          • If you will not be going into Mythic raid or high Keystone Level Mythic+ dungeons, then use Runed Harbinger Crest to craft your Staff immediately.
          • Why not craft a Main Hand and Off-Hand? While they use the same amount of Spark of Omens as a Staff would, they take twice the amount of Gilded Harbinger Crest needed to match the same strength.
        2. Craft a piece of gear in a low-impact armor slot—one whose contribution to our overall stat budget is low, like Cloak or Wrists—with the Writhing Armor Banding , in order to increase the effect of the Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension in your weapon. Using Weathered Harbinger Crest to craft an iLvL 590 piece is fine, but remember to craft a stronger version of the piece of armor at a later point.
        3. After the above crafts have been done, you'll want to shore up any weak gear slots, making sure to use Algari Missive of the Fireflash to guarantee Haste and Critical Strike as its secondary stats.

        Crafting Notes

        While we're at the start of The War Within, notable crafted gear relies on the Embellishment system, and brief descriptions of the items available to us are below. As always, make sure to use QE Live to compare your options when it comes to gearing options.

        • Waders of the Unifying Flame
          Waders of the Unifying Flame
        This item still needs testing to see what types of damage will activate it's effect, but if it works with any soruce of damage, it's an incredibly strong effect, increasing our Intellect by a decent amount while also lowering our Mastery rating, which isn't as much of a penalty for us, as it would be for other specializations. But if it only works from auto-attack damage events, it's something we won't ever equip.
        By pairing yourself with another player, you're able to trigger the Boon of Binding buff, granting each other, independently, a decent increase to each players' secondary stats, depending on the gems you have socketed.
        By going to a Scribe and having them use an existing Darkmoon Deck, you can then acquire an optional reagent that is able to be crafted with a Weapon, in order to impart that Darkmoon Deck's effect into it. There are four possible Darkmoon Sigils, ranked below:
        1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension - This provides the most secondary stats and is generally the most useful of all of the Darkmoon Sigils for Mistweavers. While it swaps between all four types of secondary stats, the amount of secondary stats it makes even Mastery a decent option to roll for us. If this embellishment tries to apply a stack of the buff to itself, and you're not in combat, it will remove all active stacks. During raid encounters, this gives us a slow start, but makes it horrible to use in Mythic+ content.
        2. Darkmoon Sigil: Symbiosis - This Darkmoon Sigil functions similarly to Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension , but is guaranteed to be Versatility. The total amount of Versatility this sigil gives is lower than the amount provided by Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension , and costs some of your health to maintain, but is a safe pick if you don't want to make two different weapons for Raid and Mythic+.
        3. Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity - This trinket relies on the schools of damage we use against our enemies, with different secondary stats being offered depending on the type of damage we deal. Because we're limited to Physical-class damaging abilities, Mistweavers aren't able to consistently get overlapping effects of this embellishment, making it incredibly weak for us.
        4. Darkmoon Sigil: Radiance - The final Darkmoon Sigil, this requires us to be dealing consistent damage to an enemy in order to both activate its effect, and to consume the effect on a single enemy. The amount of secondary stats it gives is much lower than the other options, and needing to meet the damage threshold isn't something that's consistent for us.
        These three embellishments are grouped together as a means of explaining how they function together. These are good passive options that increase a specific secondary stat, but they work on a "heartbeat" system, where the game checks your health value every 5 seconds to decide whether to apply or remove these buffs. So while it may not be updated the moment your health is raised or lowered, it's still rather consistent.
        1. Duskthread Lining - This grants Versatility while our health is above 80%.
        2. Dawnthread Lining - This grants Critical Strike while our health is above 80%.
        3. Energy Redistribution Beacon - While this embellishment looks amazing at first glance, the amount of spells it's actually applied to is rather small, to the point where you're better off using other embellishments. being able to transfer 2% of our health into direct healing sounds like a stellar increase to our healing, especially because we want to have as much Stamina and health possible because of other spells that scale from our maximum health, but it's just too limited to recommend.
        The Fury of the Stormrook set has three possible pieces that you can craft, going in the Chest, Hands, or Wrist slot. Each piece of gear takes an embellishment slot, so trying to equip this set locks you out of any other embellishments, as well as one potential Tier Set slot. If you do decide to craft this set, you'll want to craft the Roiling Thunderstrike Talons and Rook Feather Wristwraps, making sure to equip the Gatecrasher's Enduring Effigy to maintain your Season 1 Tier Set Bonus.

        The amount of Haste this set gives is quite low, especially for using both possible embellishment slots.
        While the secondary stats of these boots could be better for our raid encoutner stat priority, the effect this peice of armor gives us is...also a little underwhelming. While the amount of Haste the effect would grant would be appreciated it, having to find the player you need to come into contact with to activate it is usually going to cause too much downtime to be worth it.


        At the start of the season, acquiring each Spark of Omens has with it the possibility of creating something powerful, making early crafts extremely important for your character's power. While you want to craft gear at the highest item level possible, you can always recraft gear you've already made to get it to the highest item level possible, 636.

        While crafting gear with our Spark of Omens, we want to make sure to also use an Algari Missive of the Fireflash to give the item Critical Strike and Haste, in order to increase our throughput between both Raid and Dungeon encounters.

         Best Embellishments for Mistweaver Monks

        Mentioned in the crafted gear priority above, Embellishments are a way of adding a little bit more throughput or uniqueness to your gear. Some items naturally come with an Embellishment tag, while others require an optional reagent to be used to grant them their effects. Their impact on throughput is relatively minor, and should be considered an additional bonus, rather than anything that will make or break your effectiveness in combat.

        2. Adrenal Surge Clasp
        3. Darkmoon Sigil: Symbiosis
        4. Binding of Binding

          Your Privacy

          We process your data to deliver content or advertisements and measure the delivery of such content or advertisements to extract insights about our website. We share this information with our partners on the basis of consent and legitimate interest. You may exercise your right to consent or object to a legitimate interest, based on a specific purpose below or at a partner level in the link under each purpose.           More information

          Performance Cookies

          These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies we will not know when you have visited our site, and will not be able to monitor its performance.

          Targeting Cookies

          These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant adverts on other sites. They do not store directly personal information, but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising.

          Functional Cookies

          These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalisation. They may be set by us or by third party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you do not allow these cookies then some or all of these services may not function properly.

          Strictly Necessary Cookies

          Always Active

          These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in or filling in forms. You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the site will not then work. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information.
