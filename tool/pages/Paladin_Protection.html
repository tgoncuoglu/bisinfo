    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Protection Paladin, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Protection Paladin in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Protection Paladin in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Protection Paladin gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Protection Paladin drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        Here is the best gear for Protection Paladin from both raids and mythic+. You should always chase item level first. Item level is more important than optimized secondary stats as it gives more health, strength, and armor so you might find gear that's better than what's listed here strictly because of item level.

        It is always recommended that you use Raidbots to sim your character since every character is different.

        Best Protection Paladin Trinkets in The War Within Season 1

        It's important to know how each trinket can be used to get the most out of it. Ideally, you would have a collection of trinkets that you can swap out as you encounter different combat situations as a Protection Paladin.

        Here is a list of the trinkets in The War Within Season 1 and some notes on when/where/how it should be used. The ones not worth using are noted. The grades are a composite score that factors in damage output, utility, defensive power, and usability. As always, if you want to gear more for damage, make sure you sim your trinkets.

        You can click on the notes icons next to each item for a rundown on the reasoning!

          This is a trickle absorption trinket with an on-use that scales with Haste. In M+, you could have potentially used it to help fill defensive gaps but the shield strength is not enough. It's barely a single melee swing as key levels get higher.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Protection Paladin

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Upgrading trinkets like Ara-Kara Sacbrood or Skarmorak Shard will provide a significant bang for your upgrade bucks. As they are unique items that can't be replaced, upgrading trinkets first is never wrong since you will likely use them everywhere.
        2. Main Hand Weapon / Shield Explanation
          Main hand weapons or your shield are safe investments. Your threat/damage output is tied to your main hand so if you get a drop then upgrading it could be a good choice. Additionally, the shield slot is the most impactful defensive slot on your character sheet as it gives a large amount of armor and is the only source of the block stat. Upgrading shields is a no-brainer as a shield tank.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest, and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

        Best Crafted Gear for Protection Paladin

        Recommended Crafting Order

        1. Everforged Vambraces, Everforged Greatbelt, or Everforged Sabatons
          • Use Elemental Focusing Lens on any of these pieces. Bracer is a good first slot because of low item budget then pick belt or boots, depending on biggest upgrade.
            Note: This requires that you have one secondary stat gem equiped for it to function.
        2. Any Low Item Level Piece
          • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level.
          • It's best to simulate your character using the Droptimizer to pick out good upgrades.
        3. Algari Alchemist Stone
          • A trinket option if you have bad luck.
        It's also worth noting that if at any point you're really struggling to pick up a main-hand weapon and you have a spare spark, that will always be the strongest immediate upgrade available. If you do, make sure that it's NOT an intellect weapon, or a dagger as we can't use those. An example piece is - Everforged Longsword.

        Crafting Notes

        • Sanctified Steps
          Sanctified Steps
        • Beledar's Bulwark
          Beledar's Bulwark
        These boots give some speed, which is nice for a slow class and spec. It has a proc rate of 9 procs per minute so you'll get a damage proc every 70-90 seconds. In theory, it should have a consistent value and might be an item we want to craft. However, it was not testable during beta, so it's hard to confirm this assumption. As soon as more data is available, this space will be updated. In other words, keep your powder dry.
        This is an item to avoid. It's an embellishment in your shield slot and it will be a lower item level and value than a normal max-level item level slot. The proc rate is ~1.2 per minute and the stat gain will never outweigh the value of strength, armor, block, and stamina.

        Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

        While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Feverflare to guarantee Haste and Mastery, unless the piece has fixed stats already present or your stat balance leans you toward a different recommendation.

        Best Embellishments

          Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top, your decision on what to craft should (generally) be dictated by the slot rather than these effects with some small exceptions. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
