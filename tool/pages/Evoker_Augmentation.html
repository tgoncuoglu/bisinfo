    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Augmentation Evoker, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Augmentation Evoker in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Augmentation Evoker in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Augmentation Evoker gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Augmentation Evoker drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        As a rough guideline follow the stat priority of: Mastery = Critical Strike > Haste >> Versatility. Don't neglect the value of Intellect, a substantial increase in ilvl is generally worth it compared to optimized stats. It is always recommended that you use Raidbots to sim your own character since every character has different circumstances.

        Wings of Shattered Sorrow

        Wings of Shattered Sorrow is a Very Rare loot drop from the Rasha'nan encounter in the Nerb-ar Palace raid. This cloak has stats which are not ideal for Augmentation Evoker, and represents a small DPS loss compared with alternatives which maximize Mastery and Critical Strike. That said, this cloak also includes a defensive effect that can be useful in essentially all progression PvE content, and is generally worth the minor damage loss.

        Just note that as a Very Rare loot drop in the cloak slot, it is likely that most of your raid members will also be interested in this item, making it fairly challenging to acquire. If you are able to secure this drop for yourself, you should find it quite useful, however avoid over-prioritizing acquiring this over more substantial upgrades in higher value slots.

        Best Augmentation Evoker Trinkets in The War Within Season 1

        When aiming to maximize overall damage, Augmentation Evokers are generally most interested in trinkets with passive Intellect, a strong damaging proc, or a powerful on-use effect to pair with Breath of Eons. Trinkets that provide secondary stats can be situationally useful, provided that you are able to consistently align the stat proc with your major cooldowns.

        It should be noted that while this list ranks trinkets for their damage potential, it is not unusual for Augmentation players to instead opt for tank, healer, or support-oriented trinkets instead, particularly in high-end Mythic+, where success often comes down to being able to survive large instances of unavoidable damage.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Augmentation Evoker

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          Overall a generally safe pick to spend your crests, especially if a crafted weapon is in the cards.
        2. Main Hand WeaponExplanation
          If not going for a crafted weapon, upgrading your weapon with crests is one of the most efficient ways to raise your character's power level. Note that upgrading two-handed weapons is considered to be more crest-efficient in the long term, as it counts for both your main hand and off hand slots.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally you should do Helm, Chest and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

        Best Crafted Gear for Augmentation Evoker

          Recommended Crafting Order

          Note: I would recommend waiting until Mythic week if possible before spending your Spark of Omens as the optimal crafting path is often different for each person and depends on gear that you earned during Heroic week and from your Great Vault.
          1. 2-Handed Weapon (e.g. Vagabond's Bounding Baton, or Charged Invoker)
            • In The War Within, creating a Enchanted Gilded Harbinger Crest requires 90 Gilded Harbinger Crest, which makes the highest tier of crests much more valuable early on when compared to Dragonflight. Crafting a weapon first is generally a solid plan unless you expect to loot a Hero or Myth track weapon early in the season, in which case you might skip this.
            • For Raid: Use Algari Missive of the Peerless and Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension .
            • For Mythic+: Use Algari Missive of the Peerless and Blessed Weapon Grip .
            • Players clearing endgame content will generally replace this craft eventually with a max item level drop from Raid or Mythic+.
          2. Binding of Binding
            • Use Algari Missive of the Peerless for stats.
            • Note: If you do not play with a consistent group, you may find frequently swapping your target to be expensive and cumbersome, and may wish to skip this craft.
          3. Belt or Other Low-Budget Non-Tier Slot with Elemental Focusing Lens or Dawnthread Lining (e.g. Glyph-Etched Binding)
            • For Raid: Use Algari Missive of the Peerless (and Elemental Focusing Lens if you have open embellishment slots).
            • For Mythic+: Use Algari Missive of the Peerless (and Dawnthread Lining if you have open embellishment slots).
            • Belt is a great choice for this craft given a lack of alternatives with suitable secondary stats, but slots like Bracers and Cloak can also be considered for this purpose.
          4. Any Low Item Level Piece
            • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level.
            • Use Algari Missive of the Peerless for stats.
            • You can use Droptimizer to help pick out good upgrades.

          Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension is one of the stronger overall options for embellishments, despite the random aspect involved around around which secondary stats you receive. That said, this effect has a ramp time, taking up to 80 seconds to reach full potency. As a result, while this is a decent option for Raid, there are generally much better options to choose for Mythic+, like the Blessed Weapon Grip .

          Recommended for Raid.
          Blessed Weapon Grip is a rather straightforward effect that adapts to your stat priority by providing you a boost to your highest secondary stat. This effect isn't outrageously powerful, but it is certainly a reasonable choice when selecting an embellishment for a crafted weapon earlier in the season.

          Recommended for Mythic+.
          The crafted ring Binding of Binding is a rather strong crafted option, but involves a unique mechanic where you have to pair to a specific ally "permanently" until you decide to pay the cost to change to a different player.

          In order to gain "full value" from this crafted choice, you will need to be in the same group or raid as your chosen partner, and ideally they have also chosen you as their partner so that you can both benefit together. Similarly, if you find that you don't often group with the same players, you may find this to be a rather expensive or cumbersome crafting choice.

          Recommended for Raid and Mythic+, provided you generally play with the same players.
          Elemental Focusing Lens is a simple and straightforward direct damage embellishment, which provides consistent value for Augmentation Evokers. At the moment this effect generally outperforms Dawnthread Lining in single target encounters.

          Recommended for Raid.
          Dawnthread Lining is a passive embellishment effect with a condition that is entirely reminiscent of Blue Silken Lining from Dragonflight. In this case, it can be reasonably expected that most players will maintain decent uptime of this effect by remaining above 80% health in most content, outside of certain dangerous moments in specific encounters.

          With Critical Strike as one of the better secondary stats for Augmentation Evoker, this choice can be expected to provide solid and consistent value, and is quite flexible in terms of which slot can be crafted.

          Recommended as a solid all-around option, particularly for Mythic+.
          Darkmoon Sigil: Symbiosis is one of the stronger embellishment options for DPS specializations that can be placed on a crafted weapon, providing a steady amount of Versatility at a rather reasonable cost. When it comes to Augmentation Evoker, Versatility is quite far behind other secondary stats, severely affecting the value of this embellishment for Augmentation specifically. Nevertheless, given that there are not currently many standout alternatives, this may also be an option for your Main Hand weapon craft.

          Not recommended.
          Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity is an interesting embellishment effect given how it creates interesting interactions with trinkets and other similar effects. Augmentation Evoker is able to activate some of the secondary stat buffs on its own, but relies on a trinket like Ara-Kara Sacbrood in order to activate Critical Strike. Unfortunately, at 2 procs per minute this effect is simply not a competitive option.

          Not recommended.

          Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

          While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Peerless to guarantee Critical Strike and Mastery, unless the piece has fixed stats already present or your stat balance leans you toward a different recommendation.

          Best Embellishments

          Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top, your decision on what to craft should (generally) be dictated by the slot rather than these effects with some small exceptions. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
          1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension (Raid) or Blessed Weapon Grip (M+)Explanation
            Your choice for weapon embellishment will generally depend on whether you are focused on Raid or Mythic+ gameplay. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension has a ramping effect that is more suitable for Raid, while Blessed Weapon Grip is more suitable for Mythic+.
          2. Binding of BindingExplanation
            Another strong embellishment option for group damage as Augmentation Evoker, especially if your chosen partner is able to run this ring as well and reciprocate the buff. This is less attractive as an option however if you do not frequently play with the same players.
          3. Elemental Focusing Lens (Raid) or Dawnthread Lining (M+)Explanation
            Elemental Focusing Lens deals direct damage to foes and is the preferred single target option most suitable for Raid. Dawnthread Lining deals less single target damage but scales into AoE and is a reasonable option for multiple targets and Mythic+.
