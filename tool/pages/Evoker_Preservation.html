    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Preservation Evoker, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Preservation Evoker in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Preservation Evoker in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Preservation Evoker gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Preservation Evoker drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        As a rough guideline follow the stat priority of: Mastery > Crit > Versatility > Haste. Don't neglect the value of Intellect, a substantial increase in ilvl is generally worth it compared to optimized stats. For the most accuracy, use QE Live to help gear your character. The below set is quite healing focused which is optimal for raid and for early Mythic+ dungeons. Later in the season you might wear a more DPS-focused set or you might equip more Versatility for very high keys.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Preservation Evoker

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these to upgrade items you obtain to their maximum item level (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. Main Hand WeaponExplanation
          Weapons are your single biggest slot and so getting a high item level one as soon as possible is your priority. If you aren't planning on crafting one then spend your Crests here first.
        2. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          If you're lucky enough to grab powerful trinkets like Unbound Changeling early then they aren't bad upgrade choices since you're likely to use them for a long time. It's impossible to waste resource this way.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. As a rough order, upgrade Chest / Legs / Helm > Shoulders / Gloves / Belt / Boots > The Rest.

        Best Crafted Gear for Preservation Evoker

        Recommended Crafting Order

        Your first craft is an important choice but it's also a little messy. Here are your options:
        1. A crafted two handed weapon with Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension on it.
          • I will clear Mythic bosses or M+9s on second week of the season: Craft nothing on week 1. Craft your staff at its maximum (636) item level on week 2 with Gilded Crests. This is the highest possible value for your crest spend. The downside is that it leaves you without a craft for the upcoming Heroic / M0 week. It might also not be a viable path if you're not interested in going into Mythic raid or Mythic+9s right away.
          • I am a heroic raider or do medium keys: If you are not going to have any Gilded crests on week two of the season then it's ok to craft a weapon using your heroic crests instead.
          • Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension is much worse in Mythic+ where stacks fall off regularly. It's still a good early craft since improving weapon item level early is of significant value but you might swap to something like Duskthread Lining a month or two into the season.
          • Be very careful that you do not craft strength or agility weapons. They do not work.
        2. Craft Writhing Armor Banding on a low budget slot like bracers or cape. This will double the power of your weapon embellishment. Eventually you'll want this on a higher item level piece but for week 1 it's ok to craft this at 590 item level.
        3. Any Low Item Level Piece
          • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level.
          • It's best to simulate your character using the QE Live Upgrade Finder to pick out good upgrades.
        Many crafted items (especially embellishments) were not very functional on Beta so check back over the next few weeks before you craft anything to make sure you have the most up-to-date information. I'd recommend waiting until Season 1 release!

        Crafting Notes

        • Binding of Binding
          Binding of Binding
        • Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension
          Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension
        • Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity
          Darkmoon Sigil: Vivacity
        Binding of Binding was our best embellishment overall but it was nerfed shortly after release. I wouldn't craft it anymore unless you'd like to funnel power to a particular player.
        This is a stacking stat embellishment. You'll get one stack every 8 seconds up to a max of 10 and it'll give you a new random secondary each time it procs. All secondaries are decent or better for Preservation so this is a fairly good Embellishment. Do note though that if it tries to tick when you're out of combat then you'll lose your stacks. Impossible in raid but could happen in Mythic+.
        This embellishment procs off our damage instead of our healing but doesn't proc enough to be a competitive choice.

        Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded all the way up to Item Level 636.

        While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Peerless to guarantee Crit and Mastery as they are our best stats.

        Best Embellishments

        Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are very minor and you shouldn't expect them to contribute more than 1% or so. You can only equip two of them at once. Details about each are as follows:
        1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension *Explanation
          A decent choice that offers a lot of overall stats. You do need to keep a fairly continuous combat going to keep your stacks maxed so this should be consistent in raid but more annoying in Mythic+. Can only be put on a weapon.
        2. Dawnthread Lining *Explanation
          We're often very healthy when we're doing the majority of our healing due to our mastery, so this is a nice and consistent buff.
        3. Binding of BindingExplanation
          An acceptable option if you want to funnel power to one player. Offers no stats to you. Purely a support option.
