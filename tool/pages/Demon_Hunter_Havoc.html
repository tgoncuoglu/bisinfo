    Gear is one of the most important elements in WoW to strengthen your Havoc Demon Hunter, providing massive amounts of stats as well as armor, procs, and set bonuses.

    In this guide, we will explain how to obtain the best gear for your Havoc Demon Hunter in The War Within Season 1 and how to check if a piece is BiS, an upgrade, or just bad. This guide will help you select the best pieces of gear from Dungeons and Raids in The War Within, whether they be weapons, trinkets, or armor.

    Recommended Gear for Havoc Demon Hunter in The War Within Season 1

    What is the BiS Havoc Demon Hunter gear?

    With many different sources of gearing and loot scarcity in The War Within, targeting your absolute Best in Slot gear isn't entirely realistic; instead, we've created a Best in Slot list from the ideal Havoc Demon Hunter drops found in The War Within Season 1 from Mythic+ Dungeons and Raids to help you decide what to prioritize.
        As a rough guideline follow the stat priority of: Critical Strike > Mastery > Haste > Versatility. Don't neglect the value of Agility, a substantial increase in ilvl is generally worth it compared to optimized stats. It is always recommended that you use Raidbots to sim your character since every character has different circumstances.

        Best Havoc Demon Hunter Trinkets in The War Within Season 1

        Havoc Demon Hunters are mainly looking for one powerful on-use trinket to pair with their cooldowns, and a supplementary either proc or flat stat trinket. The stats Havoc is most looking for on trinkets are critical strike and mastery.

        B Tier
        • Mistcaller OcarinaExplanation
          Ocarina is a relatively weak trinket on its value to you as the user, however with the buff applied to all 5 players it is relatively okay as an option.

All of these take differing amounts of time to perform during your opener and can be hard to spot.

        Best Items to Upgrade for Havoc Demon Hunter

        Completing endgame activities in The War Within will award you with Harbinger Crests and Valorstones. You can use these currencies to upgrade items you obtain to their max potential (explained in this guide). Due to the more powerful items requiring the limited currency of Crests on a weekly lockout, it means your decision making early on can be difficult. In general, you want to prioritize items you've obtained at the highest upgrade track you can obtain, with the following criteria to decide on the item:
        1. Strong TrinketsExplanation
          You get Skardyn's Grace or Ara-Kara Sacbrood early these are great upgrade options. Upgrading 2 trinkets will also free up the crest requirement if you want to invest in some new options for more specific situations if you get hold of them, so this is the safest bet for early investment.
        2. Main Hand WeaponExplanation
          This is your highest priority to get high item level, but you do have an alternative option in crafting which is powerful this season as your BiS weapon due to embellishments. If you get hold of a good enough option here early, it may be better to do this and switch your Spark of Omens priority accordingly.
        3. Lowest Item Level Pieces Explanation
          Following key pieces, bringing up the item level of pieces you don't expect to get hold of at a higher item level in the near future to remove the Crest requirement is best. Ideally, you should do Helm, Chest, and Legs first due to their stat budget, but after that go off sims.

        Best Crafted Gear for Havoc Demon Hunter

        Recommended Crafting Order

          1. Everforged Warglaive
            • A relatively strong embellishment option in Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension goes on a crafted weapon, which is used as a large early power bump for the high main-hand item level at 636. Not necessarily used in BiS Setups, however very close in power to Dawnthread Lining depending on uptime. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension will lose to Dawnthread Lining in mythic+, however the relative power of embellishments is not high so this is not of great impact.
          2. A Crafted Piece (e.g. Rune-Branded Armbands)
            • Use Dawnthread Lining on this piece. Can craft on any of gloves/belt/bracers and use two of this embellishment.
          3. Any Low Item Level Piece
            • After getting priority crafts, use Spark of Omens to fill holes in your gear to increase your overall item level.
            • It's best to simulate your character using the Droptimizer to pick out good upgrades.
          It's also worth noting that if at any point you're struggling to pick up a main-hand weapon and you have a spare spark, that will always be the strongest immediate upgrade available. If you do, make sure that it's NOT an intellect weapon, a mace, or a dagger as we can't use those. An example piece is - Everforged Warglaive.




          Your Spark of Omens items are required for crafting some of the most powerful The War Within equipment, so you should spend them wisely, aiming to craft them at the highest possible item level. Note that you can always use the recrafting system later down the line if you want to upgrade pieces, so there's no need to hoard them if there's something big you can get now. Much like early in Dragonflight, these are time-gated, with you receiving one every two weeks, and can be upgraded up to Item Level 636.

          While there is a section below regarding Embellishments, your Spark of Omens crafts should aim to craft the highest impact item that's least likely to see a replacement from other sources due to their scarcity. Every craft you perform should aim to use a Algari Missive of the Peerless to guarantee Critical Strike and Mastery unless the piece has fixed stats already present or your stat balance leans you toward a different recommendation.

          Best Embellishments

            Using the optional Embellishment items when crafting your gear provides small extra benefits to the item should the craft be successful. These effects are often quite minor and mostly considered a cherry on top, your decision on what to craft should (generally) be dictated by the slot rather than these effects with some small exceptions. They are limited to two, so putting one on for your early crafts is free added performance. Details about each are as follows:
            1. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension Explanation
              A relatively strong embellishment option in Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension goes on a crafted weapon, which is used as a large early power bump for the high main-hand item level at 636. Not necessarily used in BiS Setups, however very close in power to Dawnthread Lining depending on uptime. Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension will lose to Dawnthread Lining in mythic+, however the relative power of embellishments is not high so this is not of great impact.
            2. Dawnthread Lining Explanation
              Dawnthread gives a significant amount of Critical Strike on a relatively high uptime for Havoc Demon Hunters with their good sustain. Value scales well into AoE and is just a generally good embellishment. Assuming a higher uptime than other classes, Dawnthread is theoretical best in slot for raid and will be your best in slot for mythic+ once you have the slots to recraft off Darkmoon Sigil: Ascension .
            3. Duskthread Lining Explanation
              Duskthread gives a significant amount of Versatility on a relatively high uptime for Havoc Demon Hunters with their good sustain. Value scales well into AoE and is just generally a decent embellishment, but it is strictly worse than Dawnthread Lining due to critical strike value.
